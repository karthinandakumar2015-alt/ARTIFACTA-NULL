What is Archaeology?

Archaeology is the scientific study of human activity through the recovery and analysis of material culture. The archaeological record consists of artifacts, architecture, biofacts or ecofacts, sites, and cultural landscapes.

  • Prehistory: Exploration of human history before written records.
  • Ancient Civilizations: Studying the rise of complex societies.
  • Medieval Archaeology: Uncovering the post-classical world.
  • Modern Techniques: LiDAR, DNA analysis, and digital reconstruction.
